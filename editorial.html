Editorial Guidelines:

1. Purpose of Content

All content must serve a clear purpose: to educate, simplify, or guide decision-making in financial and AI-driven business contexts. If a piece of content does not provide real value or clarity, it should not be published.

2. Audience Focus

Our content is written for business owners, startup founders, and professionals exploring AI in finance. Content must be clear, practical, and free of unnecessary jargon, and complex ideas should always be simplified.

3. Tone and Voice

Our tone is clear, direct, and professional without being overly corporate. Avoid buzzwords, exaggerated claims, and vague statements that do not add real meaning.

4. Clarity Over Complexity

Content must be easy to read and structured for quick understanding, using short paragraphs, clear headings, and simple language wherever possible.

5. Accuracy and Responsibility

All financial and AI-related content must be accurate, realistic, and transparent. AI-generated insights should never be presented as guaranteed outcomes.

6. Consistency

Maintain consistency in terminology, formatting, and writing style across all content, as inconsistency reduces trust and weakens professionalism.

7. Originality

All content must be original and based on real understanding, avoiding generic or copy-like material that lacks uniqueness.

8. User-Centric Writing

Every piece of content must focus on user value by clearly addressing what problem it solves and why it matters.

9. Review and Improvement

Before publishing, content must be reviewed for clarity, structure, and readability, and should be continuously improved based on feedback and performance.

10. Ethical Standards

We prioritize honest communication and responsible content practices, because building trust is more important than sounding impressive.
